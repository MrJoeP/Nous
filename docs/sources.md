## The Science

**5. Dopamine as reward-prediction error signal (not "pleasure chemical")**
→ Schultz, W. (2016). "Dopamine reward prediction error coding." *Dialogues in Clinical Neuroscience*.
→ https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4826767/
→ Also: Glimcher (2011), PNAS — https://www.pnas.org/doi/full/10.1073/pnas.1014269108

**6. Volkow et al — addiction involves reward, conditioning, habit, motivation, memory, executive control**
→ Volkow, N.D. et al. (2011). "Addiction: Beyond dopamine reward circuitry." *PNAS*.
→ https://www.pnas.org/doi/full/10.1073/pnas.1010654108
→ PMC open access: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3174598/

**7. 2025 JMIR study — 21,244 adults in France, compulsive smartphone use (bedtime 43%, bathroom 36.1%, driving 44.3%)**
→ EVADD study, published March 6, 2025.
→ https://mental.jmir.org/2025/1/e63431/

**8. one sec intervention — 36% dismissal rate (~1 in 3), 37% reduction in app-opening attempts**
→ Grüning et al. (2023). *PNAS*.
→ https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2213114120
→ ResearchGate mirror: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/368682271

## Notes on Source Quality

- **Claims 1–4 (market size):** Treat as directional. Roots Analysis and Grand View Research are vendor-side market intelligence firms — useful for investor conversations but not peer-reviewed.
- **Claims 5–8 (science):** Peer-reviewed. Schultz (2016) and Volkow (2011) are canonical in the dopamine/addiction literature. The Grüning/one sec PNAS study (2023) is the strongest evidence base in the product category.
- **Claim 11 (Jomo "self-funded"):** Not confirmed in any public-facing page found. The 300,000+ user figure and 2022 launch date are confirmed; funding status should be verified directly with Jomo.
- **Claims 13–15 (regulation):** Strong. All sourced from primary government/parliament sources. The EU DFA is still in legislative progress as of May 2026.
